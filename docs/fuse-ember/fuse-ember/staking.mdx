Staking on Fuse Ember will introduce a new model designed around real network activity rather than token inflation.
The goal is to transition from an inflation-based system to a sustainable, revenue-sharing model that rewards long-term participants and aligns with network performance.

Summary
The Fuse Ember staking model introduces a revenue-backed, liquid staking token (stFUSE) that rewards participants from real network activity.
This shift from inflationary emissions to revenue sharing strengthens long-term sustainability, aligns incentives across the ecosystem, and supports new DeFi integrations.
Further technical details, including staking contracts, governance structure, and delegation mechanics, will be published alongside the mainnet upgrade plan.

Overview
The upgraded network will introduce stFUSE, a liquid staking and revenue-sharing token that represents a user’s staked position.
stFUSE will allow holders to:
Earn a share of network revenue;
Auto-compound rewards;
Use their position across DeFi;
Participate in future governance.

This approach ties staking rewards directly to protocol activity and network usage, ensuring a more sustainable system than the previous inflationary model.

stFUSE Token
stFUSE is issued when users lock their FUSE tokens.
By locking FUSE, users receive stFUSE — a receipt token representing their stake.
Locking for longer periods may result in higher weight or multipliers, similar to vote-escrow models.
Holding stFUSE enables access to multiple revenue streams generated by the network. Rewards can auto-compound, be restaked, or deployed in DeFi applications.
Note: Final details on lock-up intervals and multipliers will be shared closer to mainnet launch. (Team confirmation needed)

Revenue Streams
stFUSE holders will earn a share of network revenue collected from on-chain activity. Revenue will be distributed to stakers through a dedicated rewards vault.
Confirmed and proposed sources include:
Sequencer Fees: A share of transaction fees collected by the rollup sequencer.

Network Services & Yield Products: A percentage of protocol fees from integrated staking, restaking, or RWA strategies offered on Fuse.

Foundation Incentives: The Fuse Foundation will allocate 20,000,000 FUSE to support staking rewards during the initial two years, maintaining a minimum APY comparable to historical returns on the L1 network.

Node Sale Revenue (if applicable): A portion of revenue generated from initial node or license sales may be used to boost early staking rewards. (Team confirmation needed)

Governance & Delegation
stFUSE is designed to integrate with a delegation-based governance system, allowing users to delegate their voting power to network participants.
Note: The exact role of validators or sequencers in governance (e.g., whether a DA operator model or sequencer council will exist) is under review and will be defined after the migration architecture is finalized. (Team confirmation needed)

Key Benefits
Sustainability: Rewards sourced from actual network activity, not inflation.
Liquidity: stFUSE can be used across DeFi protocols and restaking products.
Composability: Integrates with yield and staking dApps.
Governance: Aligns incentives between users, builders, and validators.

FAQ
Q1: Will I need to migrate my tokens?
No. Since the migration is full-state, existing balances, contracts, and staking positions will carry over automatically.
Q2: Will stFUSE replace current staking contracts?
Yes. stFUSE will serve as the primary staking mechanism once the upgrade is live. Migration details will be published closer to launch.
Q3: Is the APY fixed or variable?
Rewards will be variable and based on network revenue. A temporary foundation incentive will ensure a minimum baseline APY during the initial rollout.
Q4: Will stFUSE be transferable or tradable?
Yes, stFUSE is designed as a liquid token compatible with DeFi. Transferability parameters will be confirmed before deployment. (Team confirmation needed)
Q5: Can I use stFUSE in DeFi protocols?
Yes. The design allows integration with DeFi apps, restaking products, and yield aggregators.
