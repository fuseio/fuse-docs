Since 2019, Fuse has focused on making open, accessible finance practical for everyday use. The network has processed over 135 million transactions at an average cost of $0.0001, supports millions of deployed contracts, and integrates with hundreds of partners worldwide.
Now, Fuse is entering its next phase — **Fuse Ember** — an upgrade that modernizes the network stack while preserving continuity for users, developers, and businesses.

## Why We’re Upgrading

The current chain has been reliable and stable, but its underlying stack is six years old. In 2025, the environment for scaling blockchains has matured significantly.

- **Full-state migration** — the process of upgrading the entire existing network (including all accounts, balances, contracts, and history) to a new, modern architecture is now technically feasible.
- **Rollup-as-a-Service** providers have proven frameworks for secure execution.
- **zk rollups** have become the industry standard for scalable, cost-efficient, and secure blockchain design.

Fuse will therefore perform a **full-state migration**, upgrading the network in place to a modern **zk rollup** architecture.

This approach keeps everything consistent for existing users and developers:

- No new chain to bridge to.
- No manual migration steps.
- No token swaps.
- All accounts, balances, and contracts remain intact.

The result is a network that scales more efficiently, reduces operational overhead, and aligns technically with Ethereum while maintaining Fuse’s unique ecosystem and history.

# From Polygon CDK to Full-State zk Rollup

In 2024, Fuse began preparing to migrate to a Layer 2 network built using Polygon CDK, with the goal of achieving greater scalability and privacy. However, as the technology landscape evolved and Polygon shifted focus away from CDK, a new opportunity emerged.
The team reassessed the path forward and concluded that a full-state migration offers a stronger, future-proof solution.

## How this differs from the earlier plan:

- The 2024 approach involved launching a new L2 network that users and developers would bridge to manually.

- The 2025 plan upgrades the existing chain directly, preserving the entire state and removing the need for any user migration.

This shift is possible because the technology and tooling for full-state rollup migrations have matured significantly in the past year, allowing Fuse to upgrade without fragmenting its ecosystem or requiring external bridges.
The Arbitrum stack is currently the leading candidate for implementation, due to its modular design, high throughput potential, and compatibility with Ethereum security assumptions. The final decision will follow a full technical evaluation.

## What Changes and What Doesn’t

What’s Changing:

- Underlying architecture upgraded to a zk rollup.
- Improved scalability and operational efficiency.
- Modern infrastructure aligned with Ethereum standards.

What’s Not Changing:

- Balances, contracts, and history remain intact.
- The FUSE token retains its utility and supply.
- No user or developer action required.

## Summary

Fuse is upgrading through a full-state migration to a modern zk rollup architecture, ensuring long-term scalability, efficiency, and alignment with industry standards without disrupting users or developers.
